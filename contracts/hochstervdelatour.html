Hochster v. De La Tour

2 El. & Bl. 678 (1853)

Dawson, p. 59

 

Facts: The defendant hired the plaintiff on April to start work on June 1.  The defendant repudiated the contract on May 11th, and the plaintiff sued on May 22nd.

 

Issue: Is suing for breach before the date of performance premature?

 

Rule: NEW RULE!  An action for damages can be brought immediately when anticipatory repudiation occurs.

 

Analysis: The court says that you can sue after repudiation but before the date of performance.  You don’t have to sue immediately when the other party repudiates; you can wait until the performance date if you want.

 

Conclusion: The suit was not premature.
