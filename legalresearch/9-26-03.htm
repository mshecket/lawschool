Bluebooking


What goes in the parentheses after the citation?


When you cite to state courts of appeals, use the thing in parentheses next to the words “Court of Appeals” in the Bluebook.


For state court of appeals cases, don’t put a number in the parentheses except for the year. In fact, the year should be the only number in the parentheses except when you’re dealing with a Federal Circuit Court of Appeals case.


Tricky:


Joe v. Jimmy, 27 S.W.2d 12 (Mo. Ct. App. 1995).


USCCAN is in chronological order like the Session Laws (Statutes at Large). USCCAN contains committee reports.


Do we need to know which states are in which circuits? Ohio is in the Sixth circuit, California is in the Ninth Circuit, and Texas is in the Fifth Circuit. Illinois is also in the Seventh Circuit. There are 13 Circuits: 11 numbered circuits plus the Federal and DC Circuits.


Is the ALR a primary source or a secondary source? It reports selected cases and then has essays about the cases. It’s used like an encyclopedia, though it does have some primary case law. It’s on the Key number system, which is…so good.


There are five ALR editions and the federal edition.


Pub.L. No. 107-56 – Congress number, then law number.


Don’t worry about session laws and how to cite them.


If you’re looking at an Ohio regulatory issue, it’s worth your while to check the Ohio Administrative Code as well as the Code of Federal Regulations. Usually, the state and the federal government talk about the same stuff. The federal regulations override the state regulations.
