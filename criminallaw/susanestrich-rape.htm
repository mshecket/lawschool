Dressler, pp. 357-359: Susan Estrich—Rape

 

This is a real lefty, liberal, pinko article.  COMMIES!  No, seriously, this is pretty strident.  Maybe it’s right, maybe it’s wrong, but you can’t say it’s not controversial.

 

The law is sexist!  It discriminates against women.  “Boys’ rules” are applied to “boys’ fights”.  Male standards are used to judge both men and women.  Estrich suggests that women should be held to a different standard of conduct.

 

Notes and Questions

 

1.     I think the Scottsboro example makes a great case that the offense of rape is a double-edged sword, and it can be used as an exercise of power by either the (actual) perpetrator or the (alleged) victim.

2.     What is rape?  Can we get rape by adding up other torts or crimes?  If not, what’s missing?  Is rape battery plus intentional infliction of mental distress?  Or is there something more?

Do rape laws protect women’s sexual autonomy?  Or do they “construct” a social order that keeps men in a superior position?  Is all heterosexual sex rape?
